Hello! My name is Utku Melemetci. I'm an amateur programmer and filmmaker. And when I mean amateur, I really mean amateur. I'm sure the software and film pages will make that obvious.

I have been programming for a couple of years now. When I started, my intention was actually to make games. However, with time, I found that I couldn't stay motivated long enough to work on something like a game, as it involved many different skills, some of which I was not interested in. So, before even finishing I game, I gave up on game dev.

However, I didn't quit programming. I kept doing simple projects in the language I knew best, C#. Eventually, what started as a small passion became raging fire that now consumes most of my time, which is both a good and bad thing.

Last year, I joined my school's robotics club. The language of choice was Java, which obviously wasn't a difficult switch. I feel that it is there where I advanced the most in terms of general programming skills. The people in the club were very helpful an I learned a lot.

Towards the end of that year, I started making my first open source contributions, mainly to the ILGPU repository which you can read more about here. The community there was also really helpful, and I'm super grateful for having found that library. And well, that's where I am now, working on some personal projects and contributing to others' projects as well.

As for filmmaking, that started around a year and a half ago, I think. I remember being inspired by Mumbo Jumbo, a popular minecraft YouTuber but also filmmaker. I watched his short film, "The Deal", and really liked the idea. I wanted to make something like it, so I came up with a skit with two burglars, robbing a house, but one of the was an amateur and always made the worst decisions. I only got halfway through before giving up.

However, in due time, I had another idea. This time, I was inspired by a book I had read, called Refugee. I guess my film, Worth-The-Risk was supposed to be about someone with the same background, someone from a war torn country suffering every day.

I wrote and produced the film with the help of a relative who helped me a lot during shooting and production. In the end, I think it turned out alright. It definitely wasn't anything with the actors, because they were amazing, but I don't think my writing did the subject justice. I certainly hope to do better in the future.

So, that's me pretty much. Well, the part that matter to this website at least. If you have any questions, you can contact me through one of the methods listed in the contacts page. I don't know if I can help, but I can try. Thanks for reading!
